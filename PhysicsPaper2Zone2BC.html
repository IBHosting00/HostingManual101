Maximum mark: 8 Question 1 Projectile Motion
✓ Verified

A projectile is launched from ground level with an initial speed of u = 35 ms^{-1} at an angle of θ = 42° above the horizontal. Air resistance is negligible. (g = 9.8 ms^{-2})

[2] (a) Calculate the horizontal and vertical components of the initial velocity.

[2] (b) Determine the time of flight of the projectile.

[2] (c) Calculate the maximum height reached above ground level.

[2] (d) Determine the horizontal range of the projectile.

Maximum mark: 8 Question 2 Carnot Cycle
✓ Verified

A Carnot heat engine operates between a hot reservoir at T_H = 520 K and a cold reservoir at T_C = 300 K. The engine absorbs 1200 J of heat from the hot reservoir per cycle.

[2] (a) State the two isothermal processes and the two adiabatic processes in a Carnot cycle.

[2] (b) Calculate the theoretical maximum efficiency of this engine.

[2] (c) Determine the work done per cycle by the engine.

[2] (d) Calculate the heat rejected to the cold reservoir per cycle.

Maximum mark: 8 Question 3 Thermal / Phase Change
✓ Verified

A block of ice of mass m = 0.600 kg is taken from a freezer at -18.0°C and placed in a well-insulated container. It is heated until it becomes water at 25.0°C. Use the following data:

  • Specific heat capacity of ice: c_i = 2100 J kg^{-1} °C^{-1}
  • Specific heat capacity of water: c_w = 4200 J kg^{-1} °C^{-1}
  • Latent heat of fusion of ice: L_f = 3.34 × 10^5 J kg^{-1}

[2] (a) Calculate the energy required to raise the temperature of the ice from -18.0°C to 0°C.

[2] (b) Determine the energy required to melt the ice completely at 0°C.

[2] (c) Calculate the energy required to raise the temperature of the resulting water from 0°C to 25.0°C.

[2] (d) Determine the total energy absorbed by the ice block during the entire process.

Maximum mark: 7 Question 4 Refraction / Snell's Law
✓ Verified

A ray of light travels from air (n_{air} = 1.00) into a glass block (n_{glass} = 1.52). The angle of incidence is i = 35.0°.

[2] (a) State Snell's law in words and in mathematical form.

[2] (b) Calculate the angle of refraction inside the glass block.

[3] (c) The light ray now travels from the glass block back into air. Calculate the critical angle for the glass-air boundary. Determine what happens when the angle of incidence exceeds this critical angle.

Maximum mark: 9 Question 5 Projectile & Energy

A projectile of mass 0.250 kg is launched from ground level at 30.0 m s^{-1} at an angle of 50.0° above the horizontal. (g = 9.8 m s^{-2})

[2] (a) Calculate the kinetic energy of the projectile at the instant of launch.

[2] (b) Determine the gravitational potential energy of the projectile at its maximum height (relative to ground).

[3] (c) Using conservation of energy, find the speed of the projectile at its maximum height.

[2] (d) Explain why the horizontal component of velocity remains constant throughout the motion.

Maximum mark: 6 Question 6 Carnot Cycle

A Carnot refrigerator operates between a cold reservoir at -10.0°C and a hot reservoir at 25.0°C. The refrigerator removes 500 J of heat from the cold reservoir each cycle.

[2] (a) Define the coefficient of performance (COP) for a refrigerator.

[2] (b) Calculate the Carnot COP of this refrigerator.

[2] (c) Determine the work input required per cycle for this ideal refrigerator.

Maximum mark: 10 HL Question 7 AHL Topic

A solid sphere of mass M = 1.5 kg and radius R = 0.10 m rolls without slipping down an incline of length L = 1.8 m, angle θ = 40°. Moment of inertia of a solid sphere is I = (2/5)MR^2.

[2] (a) State the condition for rolling without slipping.

[3] (b) Derive an expression for the linear acceleration of the sphere in terms of g and θ.

[3] (c) Calculate the linear speed of the sphere at the bottom of the incline.

[2] (d) Compare the translational kinetic energy to the rotational kinetic energy at the bottom.

Maximum mark: 8 HL Question 8 AHL Topic

A Carnot heat engine operates between a hot reservoir at 800 K and a cold reservoir at 300 K. It absorbs 2000 J per cycle from the hot reservoir.

[2] (a) Calculate the efficiency of this Carnot engine.

[2] (b) Determine the work output per cycle.

[4] (c) If the cold reservoir temperature is decreased to 250 K while the hot reservoir remains at 800 K, calculate the percentage change in efficiency. Comment on the significance of this result.
