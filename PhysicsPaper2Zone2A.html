Physics Paper 2, Timezone 2A

Short answer and extended response questions. Answer all questions on separate paper.

⚠️ Disclaimer: Only about 75% of these questions are confirmed. The rest are likely either going to appear on the exams or not, there's no way for us to tell.

Standard Level (SL) Shows HL Questions
Section A Core SL & HL candidates answer all questions
Maximum mark: 8 Question 1 Theme A
✓ Verified

A metal block of mass m = 2.0 kg is placed on a frictionless incline of angle θ = 20°. It is released from rest at point P, which is h = 3.5 m vertically above the ground.

[2] (a) Draw a free-body diagram for the block on the incline, labelling all forces.

[2] (b) Calculate the acceleration of the block along the incline. (g = 9.8 m/s²)

[2] (c) Determine the speed of the block just before it reaches the bottom of the incline.

[2] (d) The block slides onto a rough horizontal surface with coefficient of kinetic friction μ_k = 0.30. Calculate how far it travels before stopping.

Maximum mark: 7 Question 2 Theme C
✓ Verified

A stretched cable of length L = 1.8 m is fixed at both ends. The wave speed on the cable is v = 144 m/s.

[1] (a) State the condition required for a standing wave to be established on the cable.

[2] (b) Calculate the fundamental frequency of the cable.

[2] (c) Sketch the standing wave pattern for the third harmonic (label nodes N and antinodes A).

[2] (d) The cable is now tightened, increasing the wave speed by 15%. Explain the effect on the fundamental frequency.

Maximum mark: 8 Question 3 Theme B

A fixed mass of an ideal gas is contained in a cylinder by a movable piston. Initial pressure P₁ = 1.8 × 10⁵ Pa, volume V₁ = 0.025 m³, temperature T₁ = 280 K.

[2] (a) The gas expands isothermally to 0.055 m³. Calculate the final pressure.

[2] (b) Determine the work done by the gas during this isothermal expansion.

[2] (c) The gas is then compressed adiabatically back to the original volume. State and explain what happens to the temperature.

[2] (d) Sketch the two processes (isothermal expansion and adiabatic compression) on a P-V diagram.

Maximum mark: 7 Question 4 Themes D & A

A charged particle of mass m = 4.0 × 10⁻¹⁶ kg and charge q = +2.5 × 10⁻¹⁹ C enters a uniform electric field E = 3.0 × 10⁴ N/C directed vertically upward. The particle is projected horizontally with initial speed u = 0.9 × 10⁵ m/s.

[2] (a) Calculate the magnitude and direction of the electric force.

[2] (b) Determine the vertical acceleration of the particle (ignore gravity).

[3] (c) After what horizontal distance will the particle have a vertical displacement of 0.10 m?

Section B Extended Response Answer all questions
Maximum mark: 9 Question 5 Theme A

A roller coaster car of mass 500 kg starts from rest at point A, height H = 22 m above ground. It moves along a frictionless track and completes a vertical loop of radius r = 7.0 m.

[2] (a) Calculate the speed of the car at the top of the loop (point B).

[2] (b) Determine the normal force exerted by the track on the car at the top of the loop.

[3] (c) If the track is not frictionless and the car's speed at the top is only 10 m/s, calculate the work done by friction from start to top.

[2] (d) Explain what would happen to the apparent weight of a passenger at the top if the loop had a smaller radius.

Maximum mark: 6 Question 6 Theme E

A sample of radioactive strontium-90 (⁹⁰Sr) has initial activity 3.0 × 10⁶ Bq. Half life = 28.8 years.

[2] (a) Calculate the decay constant λ in s⁻¹.

[2] (b) Determine the number of strontium-90 nuclei initially present.

[2] (c) Estimate the time required for the activity to fall to 3.75 × 10⁵ Bq.

Section C Higher Level Extension HL only AHL topics
Maximum mark: 10 HL Question 7 AHL Topic
✓ Verified

A solid cylinder of mass M = 2.5 kg and radius R = 0.12 m rolls without slipping down an incline of length L = 2.0 m, angle θ = 35°. Moment of inertia of a solid cylinder is I = (1/2)MR².

[2] (a) State the condition for rolling without slipping.

[3] (b) Derive an expression for the linear acceleration of the cylinder in terms of g and θ.

[3] (c) Calculate the linear speed of the cylinder at the bottom of the incline.

[2] (d) Compare the translational kinetic energy to the rotational kinetic energy at the bottom.

Maximum mark: 8 HL Question 8 AHL Topic

In a photoelectric experiment using a metal surface, stopping potential Vs = 0.95 V when light of wavelength λ = 420 nm is incident. (h = 6.63 × 10⁻³⁴ Js, c = 3.00 × 10⁸ m/s, e = 1.60 × 10⁻¹⁹ C)

[2] (a) Determine the work function of the metal in eV.

[2] (b) The metal is illuminated with UV light of frequency 1.0 × 10¹⁵ Hz. Calculate the maximum kinetic energy of photoelectrons.

[4] (c) Discuss how the de Broglie wavelength of photoelectrons changes as incident frequency increases. Support with calculations.
