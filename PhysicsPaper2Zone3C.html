Maximum mark: 8 Question 1 Theme A
✓ Verified

A steel block of mass m = 1.2 kg is placed on a frictionless incline of angle θ = 32°. It is released from rest at point P, which is h = 2.5 m vertically above the ground.

[2] (a) Draw a free-body diagram for the block on the incline, labelling all forces.

[2] (b) Calculate the acceleration of the block along the incline. (g = 9.8 m/s²)

[2] (c) Determine the speed of the block just before it reaches the bottom of the incline.

[2] (d) The block slides onto a rough horizontal surface with coefficient of kinetic friction μ_k = 0.20. Calculate how far it travels before stopping.

Maximum mark: 7 Question 2 Theme C
✓ Verified

A stretched thread of length L = 0.6 m is fixed at both ends. The wave speed on the thread is v = 48 m/s.

[1] (a) State the condition required for a standing wave to be established on the thread.

[2] (b) Calculate the fundamental frequency of the thread.

[2] (c) Sketch the standing wave pattern for the third harmonic (label nodes N and antinodes A).

[2] (d) The thread is now tightened, increasing the wave speed by 12%. Explain the effect on the fundamental frequency.

Maximum mark: 8 Question 3 Theme B

A fixed mass of an ideal gas is contained in a cylinder by a movable piston. Initial pressure P₁ = 5.0 × 10⁵ Pa, volume V₁ = 0.010 m³, temperature T₁ = 250 K.

[2] (a) The gas expands isothermally to 0.040 m³. Calculate the final pressure.

[2] (b) Determine the work done by the gas during this isothermal expansion.

[2] (c) The gas is then compressed adiabatically back to the original volume. State and explain what happens to the temperature.

[2] (d) Sketch the two processes (isothermal expansion and adiabatic compression) on a P-V diagram.

Maximum mark: 7 Question 4 Themes D & A

A charged particle of mass m = 3.0 × 10⁻¹⁶ kg and charge q = +1.0 × 10⁻¹⁹ C enters a uniform electric field E = 2.5 × 10⁴ N/C directed vertically upward. The particle is projected horizontally with initial speed u = 2.0 × 10⁵ m/s.

[2] (a) Calculate the magnitude and direction of the electric force.

[2] (b) Determine the vertical acceleration of the particle (ignore gravity).

[3] (c) After what horizontal distance will the particle have a vertical displacement of 0.05 m?

Section B Extended Response Answer all questions
Maximum mark: 9 Question 5 Theme A

A roller coaster car of mass 380 kg starts from rest at point A, height H = 28 m above ground. It moves along a frictionless track and completes a vertical loop of radius r = 9.0 m.

[2] (a) Calculate the speed of the car at the top of the loop (point B).

[2] (b) Determine the normal force exerted by the track on the car at the top of the loop.

[3] (c) If the track is not frictionless and the car's speed at the top is only 13 m/s, calculate the work done by friction from start to top.

[2] (d) Explain what would happen to the apparent weight of a passenger at the top if the loop had a smaller radius.

Maximum mark: 6 Question 6 Theme E

A sample of radioactive polonium-210 (²¹⁰Po) has initial activity 8.0 × 10⁶ Bq. Half life = 138.4 days.

[2] (a) Calculate the decay constant λ in s⁻¹.

[2] (b) Determine the number of polonium-210 nuclei initially present.

[2] (c) Estimate the time required for the activity to fall to 1.0 × 10⁶ Bq.

Section C Higher Level Extension HL only AHL topics — multi‑concept problem solving
Maximum mark: 12 HL Question 7 AHL Topic
✓ Verified

A solid sphere of mass M = 2.4 kg and radius R = 0.12 m rolls without slipping down an incline of length L = 3.2 m, angle θ = 25°. The moment of inertia of a solid sphere is I = \frac{2}{5}MR^2. At the bottom, the sphere collides elastically with an identical sphere initially at rest on a horizontal frictionless surface. The second sphere then swings up a frictionless circular arc of radius r = 0.80 m as a pendulum.

[2] (a) Show that the linear acceleration of the sphere down the incline is a = \frac{5}{7}g\sin\theta. Derive from Newton's laws and rotational dynamics.

[2] (b) Calculate the linear speed of the first sphere just before the collision.

[3] (c) Determine the speeds of both spheres immediately after the elastic collision.

[3] (d) Find the maximum angle that the second sphere reaches on the circular arc.

[2] (e) Discuss how the result would differ if the spheres were hollow (with I = \frac{2}{3}MR^2) instead of solid.

Maximum mark: 10 HL Question 8 AHL Topic

A square coil of side a = 0.15 m has N = 200 turns and rotates with frequency f = 50 Hz in a uniform magnetic field B = 0.32 T. The axis of rotation is perpendicular to the field. Initially the plane of the coil is perpendicular to the field.

[2] (a) Derive an expression for the magnetic flux through the coil as a function of time. Calculate the maximum flux.

[2] (b) Use Faraday's law to determine the induced emf as a function of time. Calculate the peak emf.

[3] (c) The coil is now connected to a load resistor R = 15\ \Omega. Calculate the average power dissipated. (Assume the coil's internal resistance is negligible.)

[3] (d) If the magnetic field strength is increased by 25%, while the frequency is decreased by 20%, analyze the percentage change in the induced emf.

Maximum mark: 10 HL Question 9 AHL Topic

In a photoelectric experiment, light of wavelength λ = 310 nm is incident on a metal surface with work function φ = 2.10 eV. The ejected photoelectrons are then accelerated through a potential difference V = 150 V.

[2] (a) Calculate the maximum kinetic energy (in eV) of the photoelectrons immediately after emission.

[2] (b) Determine the de Broglie wavelength of these electrons after acceleration.

[3] (c) The electrons are now used in an electron diffraction experiment with a crystal. The first diffraction minimum occurs at θ = 12.5°. Estimate the spacing between atomic planes in the crystal.

[3] (d) Compare the de Broglie wavelength of an electron accelerated through 150 V with that of a proton accelerated through the same potential. Justify your answer mathematically using the relativistic formula only if necessary (classical approximation is acceptable).
